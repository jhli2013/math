Research

I work on statistical mechanics. More precisely, I am interested in different percolation models (random-cluster model), Ising model, quantum Ising model and integrable probabilities. In particular, I try to understand the universal behaviour of such models at criticality.

Publications

Universality for the Random-Cluster Model on Isoradial Graphs
with H. Duminil-Copin and I. Manolescu, Electronic Journal of Probability (2018).
more picture_as_pdf
more_horiz
We show that the canonical random-cluster measure associated to isoradial graphs is critical for all $q \geq 1$. Additionally, we prove that the phase transition of the model is of the same type on all isoradial graphs: continuous for $1 \leq q \leq 4$ and discontinuous for $q > 4$. For $1 \leq q \leq 4$, the arm exponents (assuming their existence) are shown to be the same for all isoradial graphs. In particular, these properties also hold on the triangular and hexagonal lattices. Our results also include the limiting case of quantum random-cluster models in $1+1$ dimensions.

Simulations

Star-triangle transformation in random-cluster model

The critical random-cluster model (or Bernoulli percolation) on an isoradial graph has parameters given in terms of the local geometry. In particular, these parameters define an invariant model under the so-called star-triangle transformations. In mathematical terms, let us write $\mathbb{P}_G$ for the critical random-cluster measure on the isoradial graph $G$ and take $T$ to be a star-triangle transformation. Then the invariance tells us that $T \circ \mathbb{P}_{G} = \mathbb{P}_{T(G)}$ with equality in distribution.

By gluing isoradial graphs together, one can switch their positions by performing some sequence of such transformations, and the invariance ensures that along the sequence, the model stays critical (the underlying graphs change, however).

As such, if one manages to show that the behaviours of the model do not change too much (or change in a reasonable way) before and after such transformations, then the same would also hold for quantities of interest.
